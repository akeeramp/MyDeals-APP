Business Process and Abstract Design

High Level Business Process

At a high level, the purpose of My Deals is to track and manage the negotiation of Intel rebates to external world-wide customers. Negotiation with the customer starts at a product-by-product Pricing Strategy. Once these Pricing Strategies pass Meet Comp, Pricing Cost Test and the proper approval channels, rebate deals are created under 1 of 9 different Deal Types: ECAP, Program, Voltier, KIT, Flex, Revtier, Density, Hybrid ECAP, Hybrid Voltier. The rebate deals are then matched at the invoice level by Downstream Applications.

Scope in Business Process

Based on the High Level Business Process, My Deals needs to provide significant improvement to the current process based mainly on Data Integrity, Performance and User Experience.

Business Process Injection Points

There are different points in the Business Flow where there are opportunities for My Deals to inject value to the process.

Rebate Strategies

OUT OF SCOPE

FSE, BMT and MMBP user roles negotiate Quarterly or Yearly Rebate Strategies in an offline Excel Spreadsheet. Most negotiations take months to complete while others must be completed withing 24 hours. The Rebate Strategies vary by customer and user with the potential of having hundreds of different Excel Formats.
Note: MMBP is DA, BMT is GA.

Typical tasks include grouping products together, comparing potential rebates to the Floor Price and running analytics to help in the decicion making process. All negotiations must both be coordinated with the customer as well as Intel's Geo and Division user base.

In order to create a new Pricing Strategies either click the + on the Navigator Menu or from the current strategy or table if previously entered.

Pricing Strategies

OUT OF SCOPE

The Rebate Strategy focuses on Products and Pricing. The next step is to convert these Rebate Strategies into Pricing Strategies. The Pricing Strategy consists of Pricing Tables and Terms and Conditions about the Rebate Agreement. Currently this is a manualy conversion as decisions based on how payments will be made and the way the rebate was structure can dictate which potential Deal Type will be generated from the Pricing Table.

Pricing Table Association

IN SCOPE

The Pricing Tables should be created based on predefined Pricing Table Templates. These templates should map each field from the Pricing Table to Rebate Deal Attributes. The Pricing Table Template need to be managed in a secure admin tool.

After adding the rebate type, you will be navigated to the handsonable pricing table template where you can add the products.

Rebate Strategy Versions

OUT OF SCOPE

During the process of negotiating Rebate Stategies and creating Pricing Stratgies, back and forth communication with the Customer can result in many versions of the Rebate Strategy. Each Rebate Strategy needs to be saved because the customer might end up choosing an older version of the Rebate Strategy and the associated Meet Comp and Pricing Cost Tests run against that version.

Rebate Strategy Approval

IN SCOPE

FSE, BMT and MMBP user roles negotiate the Rebate Strategy. The system must track who and when the Rebate Strategy was approved for SOX compliance.
Note: MMBP is DA, BMT is GA.

Deal Validation

IN SCOPE

As the Rebate Strategy gets closer to completion, the resulting Draft Rebate Deals are Validated to ensure all necessary fields are populated. Some fields might be needed for Cost Test or DownStream Applications. The Deal Validation is managed by complex Business Logic that can "heal" common missing fields in the Deal. The deals can be validated in Bulk and pass or fail messages are queued and returned back to the client.

Overlapping Deals

IN SCOPE

As the Draft Rebate Deals from the Pricing Strategies are prepared, the system needs to determine overlapping deals. Overlapping deals are defined by deals that overlap in time with the same products in such a way that it is possible for the customers to get multiple rebates for the same product. The overlapping rules should be managable from an admin screen.

Overlapping deals have same customer, product, overlapping deal dates & End Customer.

Meet Comp Test

IN SCOPE

For certain products, Intel is required to ensure the product is NOT being sold for less than our competitors. Another reason for Meet Comp is to determine a reason for why a specific rate was requested.

Meet Comp Test results are displayed on the screen with color indicators.

  • Green: Meet Comp Passes
  • Red: Meet Comp Failes
  • Yellow: Incomplete Meet Comp due to required fields not being populated
  • Grey: Meet Comp is not needed

The Meet Comp Test screen shows the Meet Comp results at both the deal and the product level.

Click Here for more details on MeetComp Test

Pricing Cost Test

IN SCOPE

Pricing Cost Test is performed by MMBP against Product Cost data. The Cost Test must run against all overlapping deals in the current Rebate Strategy as well as any deal that has a tracker number. Based on the Product Group, Market Share and Standalone/Kit, various Cost Test formulas are computed against the Product Group to determine the Cost Test result.

Cost Test results are displayed on the screen with color indicators.

  • Green: Cost Test Passes
  • Red: Cost Test Failes
  • Yellow: Incomplete Cost Test due to required fields not being populated
  • Grey: Cost Test is not needed

Term and Conditions

OUT OF SCOPE

In addition to Pricing Tables, Pricing Strategy can contain Terms and Conditions related to the Pricing Table. These Terms and Conditions generally effect how the deals are created and/or effect the deals workflow. Idealy, these Terms and Conditions can be user managed and created to map the changes to the deals automatically. For the Term and Conditions that cannot be automated or simply haven't been created for the automated process, the user can manually fix the deals prior to approving them.

Contract

OUT OF SCOPE

Pricing Strategies are directly used in the formal Contract created and presented to customers. While the current process allows contracts to be created prior to entering deals in the My Deals tool, the tool needs to have the capability to cut-n-paste or export the pricing tables and Terms and Conditions in order to be added to the contract in an external tool.

Creating a brand new contract is simple. Set your role to GA or FSE, Click on the Create a MyDeals Contract below and you will be brought to the Contract Details page.

Customer Acceptance

OUT OF SCOPE

Customers formally agree to the Pricing Strategies in the form of a Contract created outside of My Deals. The Rebate Strategies are presented to the customer using C2a, DocuSign or SharePoint. At this time, the expectation is to remain outside of My Deals, but in the future, this should be integrated into the tool as an automated step.

For moving a deal from Pending to Approval, it is mandatory to provide C2a Contract #.

Deal Entry

IN SCOPE

Deal entry should be started after MMBP approves, passes Cost Test, passes MeetComp, passes Legal and the Customer accepted the Contract. At this time, deals should be autocreated based on the Pricing Table Associations and Pricing Table Templates. Since the deals have already been approved in the form of Pricing Tables, the deals can go directly to the HOLD or ACTIVE stage depending on the type of deal.
Note: MMBP is DA.

Deal Approval

IN SCOPE

Since Rebate Strategies are already approved and when deals are created they go to an accepted state, the Deal Approval is limited. Deals can move through various stages such as DRAFT, REQUESTED, HOLD, OFFER, WON, LOST, CANCELLED, SUBMITTED, PENDING and APPROVED/ACTIVE.

Note: MMBP is DA, BMT is GA.

You are able to select all (entire contract) or some of the pricing strategies for sending for approval. Customer Acceptance will determine the deal status after approvals have been made. When selecting as Customer Accepted if you have not entered a C2A ID#, or attached a copy or URL of the accepted contract you will prompted to enter this information. The deals will not go active until this information has been provided.

Data Model Object

At a high level, there are 3 major objects in My Deals

  • Rebate Strategy: The grouping of Pricing Strategies that will be approved together as one acceptance "Contract" to the customer.
  • Pricing Strategy: Hold the Pricing Table as well as Term and Conditions around the Pricing Table.
  • Rebate Deal: The collection of data that will be used to track and map paid invoices for credits and Rebate fulfillment.

Other Features

My Deals has other features that are common across the entire application.

Business Rules

My Deals has trigger points in the code where custom business rules can be applied. Some of these trigger points are PRE_SAVE, POST_SAVE, PRE_ACTION, POST_ACTION, etc...

These business rules come in 2 modes:

  • Validation: These rules return a true/false and does NOT alter data. It is used for decisions.
  • Action: These rules perform a validation step and the actions against the data depending on the true/false response.

An example of a business rule would be:

    During a Save and Approve Action, if the ECAP Price decreases... save the deal, redeal it, alter various attribute values, version Rebate Strategy, cancel the approval and notify the user the deal was redealed and not approved.

Workflow

The Rebate Strategy and Deal Strategy Approvals follow a defined Workflow process. These workflows can change based on Deal Type, Role, Stage, Action and the user entered deal attributes.

Redeal

After Rebate Deals are created, changes can be made to the deals. These changes can come in two modes:

  • Minor: Changes that do not require a re-approval. These are changes that do not alter rebate payments or Pricing Cost Test.
  • Major: Changes that do require a re-approval. These are changes that do alter rebate payments or Pricing Cost Test. Some examples are ECAP Price, Start Date, End Date, Total Volume, Ceiling, etc...

A Major change will initiate a redeal forcing the Rebate Strategy to change versions and allow changes to be made to the Rebate Strategy while not altering the current deal for downstream processing until the Rebate Strategy has been approved. Some other things to think about are redeals can split deals or replace deals, snapshot table concept controlling global or tracker based attributes, cancelling redeal and reverting back to previous version, etc...

Deal Types

Currently, all Pricing Strategies resolve into Rebate Deals. These Rebate Deals will be consumed by only 9 Deal Types:

  • ECAP
  • Voltier
  • Program
  • KIT
  • Flex
  • Revtier
  • Density
  • Hybrid ECAP
  • Hybrid Voltier
Click Here for more details on Deal types

Based on the Deal Type, the structure of the deal will be different. Some things that will be altered on the deal:

  • Attributes: Different attributes and schedules above the standard deals will need to be managed.
  • Rules: Different Rules (Validation and Action) will need to be fired based on Deal Type.
  • Workflow: Approval Workflows can change based on Deal Type and Deal Sub Type (MCP, Tender, etc...).

Role Types

Each user is assigned to a single Role Type which controls Business Rules, Security and Account Assignments. Currently, the Roles Types:

  • SA
  • DA
  • GA
  • FSE
  • FINANCE
  • LEGAL
  • CBA
  • RA

Stages

Workflows approve and reject Rebate Strategies and Deals through stages. Currently, the Stages:

  • Draft
  • Requested
  • Hold
  • Offer
  • Won
  • Lost
  • Cancelled
  • Submitted
  • Pending
  • Approved/Active

The Deal Editor provides a column called Deal Tools which allows users to make selections, add comments and attachments, delete, and hold.
